# RCB Sale Heats Up as Aditya Birla Group and David Blitzer Enter Ownership Race

The race to acquire Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) has taken a major turn as the consortium of the Aditya Birla Group and American sports investor David Blitzer has entered the bidding process, according to multiple reports. The move adds further intensity to an already crowded ownership battle for one of the Indian Premier League’s most valuable franchises.

The Birla-Blitzer consortium, which was previously linked with a potential acquisition of Rajasthan Royals, is now also evaluating a bid for RCB, creating what industry sources describe as a rapidly evolving and highly competitive sale process. Their entry is expected to significantly reshape the dynamics of the final bidding stage. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

If the consortium proceeds with a formal offer, the RCB sale is expected to become a three-way contest involving other major investment groups. One of the leading competing bids is reportedly a consortium led by Dr Ranjan Pai, supported by US private equity firm KKR and Singapore-based Temasek, while another includes Swedish investment firm EQT alongside Premji Invest. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

The franchise is owned by Royal Challengers Sports Private Limited, a subsidiary of United Spirits under Diageo, which has set a valuation target of around $2 billion for a full stake. However, early binding bids are believed to be in the $1.5 to $1.7 billion range, reflecting ongoing negotiations over the final valuation. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Market insiders suggest that Diageo is working toward a strict timeline to conclude the sale process by March 31, 2026, after which the winning consortium will move into regulatory approval stages, including clearance from the BCCI. The deal is expected to be one of the largest franchise transactions in global cricket history. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

The potential entry of the Aditya Birla Group also signals a deeper push by major Indian conglomerates into the sports investment ecosystem, with the IPL continuing to attract global private equity and institutional investors.
