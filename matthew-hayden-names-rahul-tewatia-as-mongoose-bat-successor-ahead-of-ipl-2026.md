# Matthew Hayden Names Rahul Tewatia as Mongoose Bat Successor Ahead of IPL 2026

Matthew Hayden has sparked conversation ahead of IPL 2026 after praising Gujarat Titans finisher Rahul Tewatia and suggesting that the Indian all-rounder embodies the modern-day evolution of the iconic Mongoose bat philosophy.

Hayden, who famously used the Mongoose bat during his explosive IPL career, highlighted Tewatia’s ability to clear boundaries under pressure and finish games in high-stakes situations as traits that align with the aggressive batting style the bat symbolised.

The former Australia opener, now part of the Gujarat Titans coaching setup, has been closely involved in refining the franchise’s middle-order approach. His comments reflect the team’s growing emphasis on power-hitting and impact finishing in the lower middle order.

Rahul Tewatia, known for his calm presence in tense run-chases and ability to shift momentum in a single over, has become a key tactical asset for Gujarat Titans. His performances in previous IPL seasons have earned him a reputation as one of the most reliable finishers in the league.

Hayden’s remarks also revive interest in the Mongoose bat, a once-controversial innovation designed to maximise power hitting by shortening the blade and extending the handle for greater bat speed. While rarely used in modern cricket, its influence remains symbolic in discussions around T20 hitting evolution.

As IPL 2026 approaches, Gujarat Titans continue to shape a squad identity built around aggressive intent, with Hayden’s philosophy pushing players like Tewatia into more defined match-winning roles.
