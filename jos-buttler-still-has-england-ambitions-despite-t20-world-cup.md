# [Jos Buttler Still Has England Ambitions Despite T20 World Cup Struggles](https://8jjcricket.com/news/jos-buttler-still-has-england-ambitions-despite-t20-world-cup-struggles-4h3px6)

England white‑ball skipper **Jos Buttler** has reiterated his desire to continue representing his country despite struggling for form during the ICC Men’s T20 World Cup.

Buttler, one of England’s most high‑profile limited‑overs players, endured a challenging tournament where he failed to make a significant impact with the bat, with critics questioning his future after a series of low scores.

Speaking after England’s exit from the competition, Buttler said that while the results were disappointing, he remains confident in his own ability and ambitions to contribute to the national team moving forward. He emphasised that international sport is cyclical and that top players must work through periods of adversity to return stronger.

“I still want to play for England and help this team achieve success in major tournaments,” Buttler said. “Form comes and goes — what matters is how you respond to setbacks and keep improving.”

Buttler’s remarks drew praise from former teammates and pundits alike, who highlighted his leadership qualities and experience as assets that could still benefit England in future ICC events.

Despite his struggles, the 33‑year‑old keeper‑batter has a formidable international record and remains one of England’s most decorated limited‑overs players, with numerous match‑winning innings across T20Is and ODIs.

England selectors have said they will continue to assess players on form and fitness ahead of upcoming tours and major tournaments, with Buttler expected to be part of discussions for future squad selections.
