# [Santner and Latham to Share Captaincy in New Zealand's T20I Series Against South Africa](https://8jjcricket.com/news/santner-and-latham-to-share-captaincy-in-new-zealands-t20i-series-against-south-africa-cBiXcX)

New Zealand have confirmed that Mitchell Santner and Tom Latham will share captaincy duties during the upcoming five-match T20I series against South Africa. The decision comes as the Black Caps look to manage player workloads following their campaign in the ICC Men’s T20 World Cup 2026. :contentReference.

Santner will captain the side in the first three matches of the series, which begins at Bay Oval in Tauranga on March 15. Test captain Latham will then take over leadership responsibilities for the final two matches scheduled in Wellington and Christchurch. :contentReference.

The selection panel opted to rotate leadership and squad members after New Zealand’s demanding run to the T20 World Cup final. Several players from the World Cup squad have been rested, while a number of emerging talents have been included to provide depth and fresh energy to the squad. :contentReference.

Top-order batters Katene Clarke and Nick Kelly, along with spinner Jayden Lennox, have earned call-ups for the series. Clarke and Lennox are expected to feature in the final two games, while Devon Conway will keep wicket during the first three matches before Dane Cleaver takes over for the remaining fixtures. :contentReference.

Fast bowler Lockie Ferguson will also have a limited role and is expected to play only in the second and third matches of the series. The rotation policy aims to help players recover after the busy international schedule. :contentReference.

The five-match T20I series between New Zealand and South Africa will run from March 15 to March 25, with games scheduled across Tauranga, Hamilton, Auckland, Wellington and Christchurch. :contentReference.
