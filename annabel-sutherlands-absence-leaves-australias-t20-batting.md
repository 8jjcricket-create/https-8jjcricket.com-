# [Annabel Sutherland’s Absence Leaves Australia’s T20 Batting Role Unclear Ahead of World Cup](https://8jjcricket.com/news/annabel-sutherlands-absence-leaves-australias-t20-batting-role-unclear-ahead-of-world-cup-ujN3L3)

Australia all‑rounder **Annabel Sutherland** will miss valuable T20 preparation time due to ongoing fitness concerns, raising questions about how the team’s batting order will be reshaped ahead of the upcoming ICC **Women’s T20 World Cup**.

Sutherland, known for her powerful middle‑order hitting and handy bowling, has been one of Australia’s most versatile players. However, a niggling injury sustained during recent domestic fixtures has delayed her participation in international T20 matches as selectors weigh her fitness against the demands of a packed schedule.

With Sutherland unavailable, Australia’s coaching staff are evaluating options to fill the void in the middle order. Players such as Alyssa Healy, Beth Mooney and Ellyse Perry are among the candidates to assume greater responsibility with the bat and provide stability in the crucial phases of the innings.

Selectors have also hinted at giving opportunities to emerging talents who impressed during recent white‑ball fixtures and domestic tournaments. The team will balance experience with youth as it finalises its lineup for the marquee event.

Sutherland’s absence could also open up additional opportunities for specialist batters and all‑rounders to stake their claims, particularly in teams where depth and flexibility are key.

Australia will monitor Sutherland’s progress closely in the coming weeks as the World Cup draws nearer, with final squad announcements expected shortly.
