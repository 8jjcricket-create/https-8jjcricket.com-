# [Esterhuysen and Fanning Put Queensland Finals Hopes in Danger](https://8jjcricket.com/news/esterhuysen-and-fanning-put-queensland-finals-hopes-in-danger-Hi0CeV)

Western Australia delivered a powerful performance against Queensland in the Sheffield Shield, putting the Bulls’ hopes of reaching the final in serious danger. The match turned dramatically after debutant pacer Albert Esterhuysen produced an impressive bowling spell and opener Sam Fanning anchored a dominant batting display for the visitors.

Esterhuysen made an immediate impact on his first-class debut, taking four wickets for 37 runs to bowl Queensland out for just 173. His disciplined pace bowling dismantled the home side’s batting lineup and gave Western Australia a significant advantage in the match. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Western Australia’s openers then piled on the pressure. Fanning remained unbeaten on 82 while Cameron Bancroft supported him with 44 not out as the pair built a commanding partnership. By stumps, Western Australia had reached 132 without loss in their second innings and established a lead of 169 runs. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

The result could have major implications for the Sheffield Shield standings. Queensland entered the penultimate round in second place but only narrowly ahead of South Australia, meaning a defeat could push them out of the race for the final. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

To make matters worse for the Bulls, all-rounder Jack Wildermuth suffered a quad strain during the match, adding further concern as Queensland fight to keep their finals ambitions alive. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
