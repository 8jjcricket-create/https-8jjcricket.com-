# [Ahmedabad Pitch Raises Selection Questions Ahead of T20 World Cup Final](https://8jjcricket.com/news/ahmedabad-pitch-raises-selection-questions-ahead-of-t20-world-cup-final-jSHAnB)

**The pitch at Ahmedabad’s Narendra Modi Stadium has become a major talking point** ahead of the ICC Men’s T20 World Cup final, with conditions likely to influence team selection and tactical planning for both India and New Zealand.

Reports suggest the final will be played on a **fresh centre wicket made from a mix of red and black soil**, a surface expected to provide good bounce while still favouring aggressive batting. The combination could produce a high-scoring encounter with a projected par total around the 200-run mark. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Unlike the slower black-soil surfaces sometimes seen at the venue, the higher proportion of red soil is expected to allow the ball to come onto the bat more consistently, encouraging stroke play and fast scoring. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

These conditions have sparked debate around team combinations. A truer surface with minimal turn may reduce the influence of traditional spin options while increasing the importance of seamers who can extract bounce or bowl effectively in the powerplay and at the death. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

For India, the pitch could affect decisions about the balance between spin and pace in the bowling attack, particularly with the final being played at the same venue where they experienced mixed results earlier in the tournament. Meanwhile, New Zealand may also weigh their options carefully, considering whether to prioritize pace variations or include additional batting depth.

The final marks the **first time the T20 World Cup summit clash will be played at the Narendra Modi Stadium**, the world’s largest cricket venue. The stadium previously hosted the 2023 ODI World Cup final and is expected to draw a massive crowd for the tournament’s climax. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

With both teams boasting powerful batting line-ups and versatile bowling units, the pitch conditions could play a crucial role in shaping the strategies used in cricket’s biggest T20 match of the year.
