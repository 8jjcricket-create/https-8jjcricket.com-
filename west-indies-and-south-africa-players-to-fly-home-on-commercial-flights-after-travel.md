# [West Indies and South Africa Players to Fly Home on Commercial Flights After Travel Delays](https://8jjcricket.com/news/west-indies-and-south-africa-players-to-fly-home-on-commercial-flights-after-travel-delays-e5eRfA)

West Indies and South Africa players stranded in India after the ICC Men’s T20 World Cup are finally set to return home on commercial flights after repeated delays to a planned charter flight organized by the International Cricket Council (ICC).

The teams had been waiting in Kolkata after their tournament exits, with travel arrangements disrupted by international aviation restrictions linked to the ongoing crisis in the Gulf region. The disruptions caused airspace closures and flight rerouting, making it difficult to organize charter flights for departing teams. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

The ICC had originally arranged a charter flight that was supposed to transport both teams from Kolkata to Johannesburg and then onward to the Caribbean. However, the flight was cancelled multiple times due to logistical issues and airport restrictions affecting charter operators. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Cricket West Indies (CWI) confirmed that the squad had remained in India for nine days waiting for confirmation of the charter flight before the board decided to secure commercial travel arrangements instead. Some players and staff members have already departed, while the remaining members are scheduled to fly home in separate groups. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

South Africa’s contingent is also expected to leave India in stages, with the ICC stating that all members of the team should depart within approximately 36 hours once the commercial travel arrangements are completed. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

The prolonged delay sparked frustration among players and officials. Several South African players, including Quinton de Kock and David Miller, publicly questioned why other teams were able to return home earlier while their squads remained stranded. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

The ICC has rejected claims of unfair treatment, stating that all travel decisions were based on safety considerations and the complex aviation situation caused by the crisis in the Middle East. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Despite the travel complications, the governing body said it continues to work with airlines and authorities to ensure that all players and support staff return home safely as quickly as possible.
