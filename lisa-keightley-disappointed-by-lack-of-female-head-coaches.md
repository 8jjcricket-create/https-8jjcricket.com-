# [Lisa Keightley Disappointed by Lack of Female Head Coaches in Women’s Hundred](https://8jjcricket.com/news/lisa-keightley-disappointed-by-lack-of-female-head-coaches-in-womens-hundred-38cH1Y)

MI London head coach Lisa Keightley has voiced disappointment at the lack of female head coaches in the Women’s Hundred, highlighting the need for greater representation in top coaching positions.

Keightley was the only woman among the eight teams’ head coaches during the recent Women’s Hundred auction. While the event marked a significant milestone for women’s cricket with record-breaking player salaries and increased investment in the sport, the absence of female leadership in coaching roles stood out.

Speaking after the auction, Keightley said it was disappointing to look around the room and realise she was the only female head coach involved in the competition. She expressed hope that the situation would change in the coming years as more women pursue coaching careers and gain opportunities at the highest level.

Despite her concerns about representation, Keightley acknowledged that women’s cricket is progressing rapidly. The growth of major T20 competitions in countries such as India, England and Australia has contributed to rising player salaries and increased professionalism within the women’s game.

Keightley encouraged aspiring female coaches to be confident and take opportunities when they arise. She noted that many women often hesitate to step into leadership roles, but believes the only way to succeed is to be brave and back themselves when offered a chance.

Other former international players are already building coaching experience in the tournament. England legend Sarah Taylor has been working as an assistant coach, while former England bowler Anya Shrubsole has also taken up a supporting coaching role.

Keightley believes that although progress may take time, the next few years could see more women stepping into major coaching positions as the women’s game continues to grow.

The 2026 season of The Hundred is scheduled to begin on July 21 and will run until August 16, featuring eight women’s teams competing across England and Wales.
