# [ECB Cancels Lions Tour Over Shocking Gulf Security Crisis](https://8jjcricket.com/news/ecb-cancels-lions-tour-over-shocking-gulf-security-crisis-eHDS8u)

The England and Wales Cricket Board (ECB) has cancelled the remaining matches of the England Lions’ tour in the United Arab Emirates amid rising security tensions across the Gulf region. The decision came after a period of instability triggered by military strikes and retaliatory actions in the Middle East, which led to heightened risk assessments for touring teams and widespread travel disruption. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

A second 50-over match between the England Lions and the Pakistan Shaheens in Abu Dhabi was called off, and all remaining fixtures were shelved as a precaution. The ECB also delayed a planned training camp for the England Women’s squad that was due to begin in Abu Dhabi, with the board working on alternative arrangements to ensure the women’s preparations continue safely. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Airspace closures across the Gulf, including in the UAE, disrupted flights and left some players and staff stranded in the region. The ECB emphasised that the safety and security of players and staff was its top priority in making the decision and is coordinating with government and security advisers on repatriation efforts. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Despite the turmoil affecting the Lions tour, England’s senior men’s team continued with its schedule at the ICC Men’s T20 World Cup, travelling for their semi-final fixture as planned while the board monitors developments in the region. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
