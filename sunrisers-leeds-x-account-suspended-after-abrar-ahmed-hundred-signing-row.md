# [Sunrisers Leeds X Account Suspended After Abrar Ahmed Hundred Signing Row](https://8jjcricket.com/news/sunrisers-leeds-x-account-suspended-after-abrar-ahmed-hundred-signing-row-fxNU4K)

The official **Sunrisers Leeds X (formerly Twitter) account** was suspended shortly after the franchise signed **Pakistan mystery spinner Abrar Ahmed** during **The Hundred 2026 player auction**, triggering widespread debate across social media.

Abrar’s signing quickly became a major talking point because the franchise is owned by the **Sun Group**, which also controls the **IPL team Sunrisers Hyderabad**. Some fans questioned the decision due to the long-standing absence of Pakistani players from the **Indian Premier League**, where they have not participated since 2009 amid ongoing political tensions between India and Pakistan. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

The controversy intensified online, with sections of social media urging others to report the franchise account after the signing was announced. The X account subsequently became inaccessible before later reappearing with its follower count reset, further fueling speculation and discussion among cricket fans. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Abrar Ahmed was signed for **£190,000** after Sunrisers Leeds outbid rival franchises in the auction. His acquisition also marked a notable moment in the tournament’s history, as he became the **first Pakistani player to join an Indian-owned team in The Hundred**. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Despite the backlash, cricket authorities have maintained that **player selections in The Hundred are based on merit and performance rather than nationality**, with the England and Wales Cricket Board reiterating its commitment to inclusivity and non-discrimination in the competition. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
