# Liam Livingstone Speaks Out as England Exclusion Sparks Management Debate

England all‑rounder Liam Livingstone has publicly voiced his frustration after being dropped from the England cricket team, claiming a lack of meaningful support and communication from the England and Wales Cricket Board’s management since his exclusion following the 2025 ICC Champions Trophy in Pakistan. Livingstone described being informed of his omission in a brief phone call from head coach Brendon McCullum and said he received little follow‑up or explanation from senior management, leaving him feeling “ignored” and disappointed. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

The 32‑year‑old, who has played in over 100 limited‑overs matches for England, revealed that after the initial notification he struggled to get clarity on his future with the national side, saying communication broke down entirely despite his attempts to engage with decision‑makers. This treatment has fueled criticism of the team’s management culture and raised questions about how players are supported when outside the core squad. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Livingstone’s comments have sparked discussion among fans and pundits about the England setup’s approach to player welfare and transparency. Some analysts argue that experienced players deserve clearer communication and constructive feedback when they fall out of favour, especially when they have contributed significantly over the years. Others have suggested that Livingstone’s outspoken stance highlights broader internal tensions within the current regime. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Despite the criticism, Livingstone has reiterated his passion for playing for England and his desire to return to international cricket, emphasising that he still believes he has his best years ahead and the ability to contribute in major tournaments. His remarks come as England prepare for upcoming white‑ball fixtures, with selectors soon to finalise plans ahead of the 2026 ICC Men’s T20 World Cup in India and Sri Lanka. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
