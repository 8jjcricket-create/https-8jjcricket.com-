# Faf du Plessis Explains How Powerplay Approach Has Changed in Modern T20 Cricket

Former South Africa captain :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0} has shared insights into how the powerplay phase in modern T20 cricket has evolved dramatically over the years. According to du Plessis, teams now require far more aggressive scoring rates in the opening overs compared with previous eras.

Du Plessis explained that the modern T20 game demands attacking intent from the very start. He pointed out that maintaining a strike rate around **120 in the powerplay is no longer sufficient**, emphasizing that teams now aim for strike rates closer to **160, 180, or even 200** to maximize the advantage of fielding restrictions. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

The powerplay, which consists of the first six overs of a T20 innings, allows only two fielders outside the 30-yard circle. As a result, batting teams often target boundaries and quick runs to build early momentum and put pressure on bowlers.

Du Plessis noted that the evolution of T20 strategies, improved batting techniques, and fearless approaches from younger players have accelerated scoring patterns across leagues and international tournaments.

## Aggressive Powerplay Strategy in Modern T20

The rise of franchise leagues and data-driven strategies has reshaped how teams approach the opening overs. Batters are encouraged to take calculated risks and exploit gaps before field placements become more defensive later in the innings.

This shift has also influenced team selection, with franchises prioritizing explosive openers capable of maximizing the powerplay advantage.

## Changing Expectations for Top-Order Batters

Top-order players now carry greater responsibility to start innings quickly. Teams increasingly expect openers to set the tempo rather than simply preserve wickets during the early overs.

Du Plessis’ comments reflect a broader trend in T20 cricket, where attacking play and rapid scoring are becoming essential components of winning strategies.
