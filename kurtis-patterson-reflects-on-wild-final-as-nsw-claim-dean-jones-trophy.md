# [Kurtis Patterson Reflects on Wild Final as NSW Claim Dean Jones Trophy](https://8jjcricket.com/news/kurtis-patterson-reflects-on-wild-final-as-nsw-claim-dean-jones-trophy-VGbYFA)

Kurtis Patterson reflected on a dramatic and rain-affected One-Day Cup final after guiding New South Wales to victory over Tasmania and securing the Dean Jones Trophy.

The final in Hobart was heavily disrupted by rain and reduced to a shortened contest, creating tense conditions for both sides. Tasmania struggled early with the bat and were bowled out for just 118 after a damaging collapse that left them reeling at 5-25 in the opening stages.

New South Wales’ bowling attack set the tone for the match. Ben Dwarshuis and Sean Abbott produced key spells to dismantle the Tigers’ batting lineup and ensure the target remained manageable for the Blues.

However, the chase was far from straightforward. NSW lost early wickets and were under pressure at multiple points in the innings. Patterson anchored the chase with a composed and unbeaten 52 from 57 balls, keeping his side calm during the tense pursuit.

The experienced batter received valuable support from Joel Davies, whose quick cameo helped shift the momentum back toward New South Wales. The Blues eventually reached the target with 11 balls to spare to secure a four-wicket victory.

The win marked New South Wales’ first One-Day Cup title since the 2020-21 season and their 13th overall triumph in Australia’s domestic one-day competition.

Patterson’s performance capped a superb tournament campaign in which he was among the competition’s leading run-scorers. After the match, he described the final as “wild” due to the weather delays, shifting momentum and the pressure of a shortened chase.

The victory ensured that NSW lifted the Dean Jones Trophy after a dramatic finale and confirmed Patterson’s outstanding influence on the tournament.
