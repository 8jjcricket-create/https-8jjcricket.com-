# [IPL Injuries 2026: Major Blow for Teams Ahead of Season](https://8jjcricket.com/news/ipl-injuries-2026-major-blow-for-teams-Cz8Qrr)

IPL 2026 is already being shaped by a growing injury crisis, with several teams suffering major setbacks even before the tournament begins.

Franchises across the league are dealing with key player absences, particularly in their pace bowling departments. Teams such as Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR), Sunrisers Hyderabad (SRH), Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB), and Delhi Capitals (DC) have all been impacted by injuries or workload-related unavailability.

KKR have been among the hardest hit, with multiple fast bowlers sidelined. Harshit Rana has been ruled out due to injury, while Matheesha Pathirana is expected to miss the early phase of the tournament. Additionally, Akash Deep has also been ruled out, further weakening their pace attack.

Sunrisers Hyderabad are facing a leadership and bowling challenge with captain Pat Cummins unavailable for the opening matches, creating both tactical and squad balance issues.

Royal Challengers Bengaluru are dealing with uncertainty around Josh Hazlewood’s fitness, which could affect their bowling plans in the early games.

Delhi Capitals are also impacted, with Mitchell Starc set to miss the start of the season due to workload management, reducing their strength with the new ball.

Elsewhere, Rajasthan Royals are expected to miss the services of Sam Curran due to injury, adding to the growing list of concerns across teams.

A noticeable trend in IPL 2026 is that most injuries are affecting fast bowlers, largely due to heavy international schedules and the physical demands of pace bowling. Teams are increasingly cautious with workload management, which is leading to players missing the early stages of the tournament.

These injury concerns could significantly influence early match results, as teams struggle to find the right combinations and replace key players. In a tightly contested tournament, even small disruptions can impact momentum and ultimately affect playoff qualification.
