# [Ibrahim Zadran Named Afghanistan T20I Captain Ahead of Sri Lanka Series](https://8jjcricket.com/news/ibrahim-zadran-named-afghanistan-t20i-captain-ahead-of-sri-lanka-series-fRFPVE)

**Afghanistan have appointed Ibrahim Zadran as their new T20I captain**, marking a significant leadership change ahead of the upcoming white-ball series against Sri Lanka.

Zadran replaces **Rashid Khan**, who previously led Afghanistan in the shortest format. The decision was confirmed by the Afghanistan Cricket Board as part of the squad announcement for the series, which will be played in the United Arab Emirates. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

The leadership change follows Afghanistan’s early exit from the **ICC Men’s T20 World Cup 2026**, where the team failed to progress beyond the group stage. Zadran had been serving as vice-captain in T20 internationals and now steps into the role as the full-time skipper. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

The upcoming series against Sri Lanka is scheduled to begin in mid-March and will include **three T20 internationals and three One Day Internationals**, providing Zadran with his first opportunity to lead the national team in the format. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Afghanistan’s squad for the T20I series features several experienced players including Rahmanullah Gurbaz, Mohammad Nabi, Rashid Khan, and Mujeeb Ur Rahman. The mix of youth and experience reflects the team’s intention to rebuild and strengthen its squad ahead of future international tournaments.
