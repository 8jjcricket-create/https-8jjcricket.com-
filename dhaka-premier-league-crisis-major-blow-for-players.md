# Dhaka Premier League Crisis: Major Blow for Players

The **Dhaka Premier League**, one of Bangladesh’s most important domestic cricket competitions, has been thrown into turmoil in 2026 due to a prolonged dispute between the **Bangladesh Cricket Board (BCB)** and several Dhaka‑based clubs. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

At the center of the crisis is a **standoff over club participation and governance legitimacy**, which has left the **Dhaka Premier Division Cricket League and other divisions stalled** for months. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1} Clubs argue that the current league administration lacks legitimacy following contested board elections, and many have refused to sit with the Cricket Committee of Dhaka Metropolis (CCDM) to finalize fixtures and season plans. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

As a result, **matches across multiple tiers of Dhaka league cricket have not taken place**, despite domestic competitions typically acting as the backbone of Bangladesh’s cricket structure. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

The main toll of this prolonged disruption has been borne by the players themselves. The **Cricketers’ Welfare Association of Bangladesh (CWAB)** has warned that with the league halted, many professional cricketers are now facing **serious financial hardship — losing their primary source of income and struggling to support their families**. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4} CWAB president Mohammad Mithun has publicly voiced frustration over the stalemate and appealed to the BCB leadership to resolve the situation and resume the leagues. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

While efforts at mediation continue behind the scenes, the **uncertainty looms large for over a thousand players**, with no clear start date for the Dhaka Premier League as negotiations remain tense and unresolved. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
