# [Abrar Ahmed Signed by Sunrisers Leeds, Becomes First Pakistani Player in Indian-Owned Hundred Team](https://8jjcricket.com/news/abrar-ahmed-signed-by-sunrisers-leeds-becomes-first-pakistani-player-in-indian-owned-hundred-team-Uxovxd)

Pakistan mystery spinner Abrar Ahmed has made history after being signed by Sunrisers Leeds during the men’s player auction for The Hundred. The deal makes him the first Pakistani cricketer to join an Indian-owned franchise in the competition.

Sunrisers Leeds secured the 27-year-old spinner for £190,000 after a competitive bidding process during the auction. The franchise, which is owned by the Sun Group and plays its home matches at Headingley in Leeds, was determined to strengthen its spin options ahead of the upcoming tournament.

Abrar’s signing was particularly notable because of earlier speculation that Indian-owned teams in The Hundred might avoid selecting Pakistani players due to political tensions between India and Pakistan. The England and Wales Cricket Board (ECB) had previously insisted that players would not be excluded from the competition based on nationality.

The talented leg-spinner has been one of Pakistan’s most exciting bowling prospects in recent years. Since making his international debut, Abrar has impressed with his variations and ability to take wickets in the middle overs, particularly in T20 cricket.

His move to Sunrisers Leeds represents a significant moment for The Hundred, as it highlights the tournament’s growing international reach and the willingness of franchises to select players purely on cricketing merit.

The signing also sparked considerable discussion among fans and analysts, given the rarity of Pakistani players appearing in teams connected to Indian franchises in global T20 leagues.

With the 2026 season of The Hundred scheduled to run from July to August, Abrar Ahmed is expected to play a key role in Sunrisers Leeds’ bowling attack as the team aims to challenge for the title.
