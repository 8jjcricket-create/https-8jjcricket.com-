# [Pakistan File Complaint Over Bangladesh’s Controversial DRS Review in Mirpur ODI](https://8jjcricket.com/news/pakistan-file-complaint-over-bangladeshs-controversial-drs-review-in-mirpur-odi-pCs7Lk)

Pakistan have filed a formal complaint with match officials following a controversial Decision Review System (DRS) decision during the first ODI against **Bangladesh** at Sher‑e‑Bangla National Stadium in **Mirpur**.

The incident occured when Pakistan’s top batter was given out LBW on the field. Pakistan used their review hoping to overturn the decision, but the third umpire ruled in favour of the ‘out’ call despite inconclusive UltraEdge and ball‑tracking data. The dramatic reversal sparked immediate debate among players and coaching staff from both teams.

Team management said that the DRS outcome “lacked consistency” and did not reflect the available technology, prompting Pakistan to submit a complaint to the on‑field umpires and match referee. Pakistan head coach Mike Hesson said decisions like this can have a “huge influence on the flow of the game” and stressed the importance of accurate use of technology in international cricket.

Bangladesh captain Shakib Al Hasan defended the officials, noting that umpires must make decisions based on the evidence presented rather than popular opinion. He added that all players understand the nuances of modern review systems and that such outcomes can happen even when technology seems to favour one side.

Spectators and fans took to social media immediately after the match to voice frustration at the call, with many saying that the reversal changed the momentum of Pakistan’s innings and potentially affected the outcome of the contest.

The ICC stated that it would review the protest and ensure that all DRS protocols were followed correctly, reiterating its commitment to uphold fairness and accuracy in the application of technology across international matches.
