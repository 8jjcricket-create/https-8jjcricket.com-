# [Salman Ali Agha Calls for Sportsmanship After Controversial Run‑Out Incident](https://8jjcricket.com/news/salman-ali-agha-calls-for-sportsmanship-after-controversial-run-out-incident-0LFXnV)

Pakistan all‑rounder Salman Ali Agha has called for greater emphasis on sportsmanship and fair play after being involved in a controversial run‑out incident that drew criticism from fans and pundits.

The incident occurred during the second ODI against Zimbabwe, when Agha was run out in a tight exchange that left spectators debating whether the fielding side’s actions aligned with the spirit of the game. Replays showed the batter slowing mid‑run to adjust his balance, only for the fielder to break the stumps with Agha short of his ground. The decision was upheld by the third umpire.

After the match, Agha addressed the controversy, stating that while he accepted the umpire’s call, he was disappointed that such incidents detract from the essence of fair competition. He urged players from both sides to “compete hard but with respect,” and highlighted that cricket’s values of respect and integrity should always guide on‑field decisions.

“I don’t dispute the decision — that’s up to the officials,” Agha said. “But I hope we can all remember that cricket is a gentleman’s game, and we should play it in that spirit.”

Former international players and commentators echoed his sentiment, noting that similar incidents in past matches have sparked debates about balancing competitive instincts with sportsmanlike conduct.

Despite the controversy, Pakistan secured a narrow victory in the match, with contributions from both bat and ball helping their bowling attack restrict Zimbabwe to a modest total.

Agha’s comments have since fuelled discussions on social media and among cricket forums about how the laws of the game intersect with unwritten codes of conduct.
