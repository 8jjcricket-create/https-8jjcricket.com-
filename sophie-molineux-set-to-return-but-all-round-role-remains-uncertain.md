# [Sophie Molineux Set to Return but All-Round Role Remains Uncertain Ahead of T20 World Cup](https://8jjcricket.com/news/sophie-molineux-set-to-return-but-all-round-role-remains-uncertain-ahead-of-t20-world-cup-YBwfq1)

Australia captain **Sophie Molineux** is expected to return to action ahead of the ICC Women’s T20 World Cup, but uncertainty remains over how much she will contribute as an all-rounder due to ongoing fitness concerns.

Molineux has been managing a back injury in recent weeks, which ruled her out of parts of the multi-format series against India and limited her preparation leading into the World Cup. Despite this setback, selectors have included her in the squad for the upcoming white-ball tour of the West Indies as part of her return to full fitness. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

While she is available to captain the side, team management are expected to carefully manage her workload, particularly her bowling, to avoid aggravating the injury ahead of the global tournament. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Molineux’s dual role as a key spinner and middle-order batter has been central to Australia’s balance, but her current condition means the team may need to adjust its combinations. This could open opportunities for other all-rounders and specialist players to step up in her absence from certain roles.

Australia are using the West Indies tour as crucial preparation for the T20 World Cup, with team management focused on ensuring players are fully fit and in peak condition before the tournament begins.

As the World Cup approaches, Molineux’s fitness and role clarity will remain a key talking point, with selectors aiming to strike the right balance between caution and competitiveness.
