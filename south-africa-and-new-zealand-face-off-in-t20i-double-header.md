# [South Africa and New Zealand Face Off in T20I Double‑Header](https://8jjcricket.com/news/south-africa-and-new-zealand-face-off-in-t20i-double-header-hGtyFU)

The **South Africa men’s and women’s cricket teams** have begun a unique **T20I double‑header tour** in **New Zealand**, where both sides play back‑to‑back matches on the same day against their respective New Zealand counterparts — the **Black Caps** and **White Ferns**. This five‑match series, running from **March 15 to March 25, 2026**, sees both men’s and women’s fixtures scheduled as double‑headers at major venues including **Bay Oval, Seddon Park, Eden Park, Sky Stadium, and Hagley Oval**. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

The opening day of action saw both sides get underway at **Bay Oval in Mount Maunganui**, with New Zealand’s women and men facing South African opposition in separate T20Is — part of an innovative format that gives fans **two international matches on the same ticket**. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

South Africa’s men, featuring a mix of experienced and new faces, are contesting for early momentum in the series, while the women’s side look to bridge the gap after recent competitive outings against top T20 nations. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Fans in New Zealand have been treated to exciting cricket over the opening weekend as the double‑header format continues at each stop on the tour, marking a significant step in staging high‑value T20 cricket in the region. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
