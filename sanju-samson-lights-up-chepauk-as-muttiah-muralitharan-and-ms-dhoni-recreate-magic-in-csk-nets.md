# Sanju Samson Lights Up Chepauk as Muttiah Muralitharan and MS Dhoni Recreate Magic in CSK Nets

Sanju Samson produced a composed and technically sharp batting display during a Chennai Super Kings training session at Chepauk, where MS Dhoni and Muttiah Muralitharan created a high-pressure, match-like environment in the nets ahead of IPL 2026.

Facing Muralitharan’s legendary spin variations, Samson initially played cautiously as the former Sri Lanka great mixed flight, pace, and subtle changes in length to test the batter’s decision-making. The session quickly developed into a tactical battle rather than a routine practice drill.

MS Dhoni, stationed behind the stumps, played an active role throughout the session, constantly communicating with bowlers and reacting to every passage of play. His presence added an additional layer of intensity, making the nets feel closer to a competitive match scenario rather than training.

At one stage, Samson was beaten while attempting to step down the track, with Dhoni positioned perfectly for a potential stumping opportunity. However, the CSK legend opted to let the moment pass, instead offering feedback and encouragement, maintaining the training rhythm while still reinforcing execution standards.

Muralitharan continued to dominate key phases of the session with controlled spin bowling, repeatedly drawing Samson into uncomfortable positions before varying his pace and trajectory. The consistency of pressure highlighted why he remains one of the most influential spin bowlers in cricket history.

Despite the challenges, Samson eventually found his timing and confidence, stepping into a delivery and driving it cleanly down the ground. The shot shifted the energy of the session, earning acknowledgment from those observing the drill and showcasing his ability to adapt under sustained pressure.

The interaction between established legends and modern IPL stars reflected the evolving CSK environment, where experienced figures like Dhoni and Muralitharan continue to shape high-performance standards while testing emerging and established players alike.
