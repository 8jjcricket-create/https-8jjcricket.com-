# [Will O’Rourke Nears Return From Back Injury as New Zealand Manage Pace Resources Carefully](https://8jjcricket.com/news/will-orourke-nears-return-from-back-injury-as-new-zealand-manage-pace-resources-carefully-ylvZRc)

New Zealand fast bowler Will O’Rourke is edging closer to a return to competitive cricket after recovering from a stress fracture in his lower back.

The 24-year-old has been sidelined since suffering the injury during the first Test against Zimbabwe in 2025. Medical scans confirmed the stress fracture, forcing him to undergo a lengthy rehabilitation programme and miss several international series.

New Zealand Cricket officials have taken a cautious approach with O’Rourke’s recovery, prioritising long-term fitness rather than rushing him back into action. The young pacer is expected to return through either New Zealand A fixtures or upcoming international tours once he is fully cleared.

Team management has been particularly mindful of managing the workload of the country’s pace bowlers. With a busy international calendar and multiple formats to balance, selectors are rotating players and limiting overs to avoid further injuries within the fast-bowling group.

O’Rourke made an impressive start to his international career before the injury setback, quickly establishing himself as one of New Zealand’s most promising fast bowlers. His height, bounce and ability to generate pace made him a key option in both Test and limited-overs formats.

The Black Caps’ pace attack currently includes several experienced bowlers alongside emerging talents, allowing selectors to manage workloads carefully while players recover from injuries.

If O’Rourke’s recovery continues on schedule, he could soon rejoin the national setup and strengthen New Zealand’s pace resources ahead of upcoming international commitments.
