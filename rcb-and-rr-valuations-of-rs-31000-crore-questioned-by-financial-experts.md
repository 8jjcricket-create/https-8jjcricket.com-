# RCB and RR Valuations of Rs 31,000 Crore Questioned by Financial Experts

The extraordinary combined valuation of roughly Rs 31,000 crore for Indian Premier League franchises Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) and Rajasthan Royals (RR) following their sales has drawn scrutiny from financial experts, who argue that such pricing may not align with traditional revenue‑based valuation logic. The record‑setting figures, largely driven by intense investor demand and scarcity of franchise assets, represent some of the most significant single‑day transactions in global sports history. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Analysts like Santosh N, managing partner at D&P Advisory, have described these valuations as “very difficult to understand,” noting that they vastly outstrip current franchise revenue streams. Estimated annual revenues for RCB and RR, even when accounting for central pool distributions, merchandising, sponsorships, and ticketing, are generally in the range of several hundred crores — a fraction of the implied multiples emerging from the sale prices. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

One reason valuations have exploded is the simple economics of IPL franchise scarcity: with only ten teams in the league and no new franchises immediately forthcoming, ownership stakes have become intensely competitive assets for global institutional investors. While deals of this magnitude are unprecedented in cricket, they mirror trends seen in other major sports leagues where elite franchises command premium prices in anticipation of long‑term media rights growth, sponsorship expansion, and global fan engagement. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

This debate comes amid broader discussions about the IPL’s commercial trajectory. Previous reports noted that franchise bids were expected in the range of roughly ₹11,000‑₹16,300 crore before the deals materialised, illustrating how actual sale figures have ballooned far beyond earlier market expectations. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Sceptics argue that while RCB and RR carry significant intangible value — driven by strong fan bases and brand recognition — the sharp rise in valuations may be more reflective of speculative investor confidence than current financial fundamentals. Much of this discussion rests on the view that future media rights renewals, global audience growth, and sustained commercial expansion will need to justify these premium prices over the long term. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
