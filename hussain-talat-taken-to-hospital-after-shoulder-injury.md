# [Hussain Talat Taken to Hospital After Shoulder Injury During Bangladesh ODI](https://8jjcricket.com/news/hussain-talat-taken-to-hospital-after-shoulder-injury-during-bangladesh-odi-m2cInb)

Pakistan all‑rounder Hussain Talat was taken to hospital after sustaining a significant shoulder injury during the first ODI against Bangladesh in Dhaka.

Talat appeared to dislocate his right shoulder while attempting a diving save in the outfield late in Pakistan’s bowling innings. The fielding effort, which came at a crucial moment in the game, saw Talat land awkwardly and immediately signal for medical attention. He was subsequently stretchered off and transported to a local hospital for scans and further treatment.

Team management confirmed that Talat was conscious and in stable condition after being examined by medical staff. Initial assessments suggested that he could be sidelined for several weeks, depending on the severity of the injury and the outcome of further scans.

Pakistan’s team physio said that while Talat was frustrated by the setback, the medical team would focus first on his recovery and rehabilitation plan. “It’s disappointing for Hussain, but we need to make sure his shoulder heals properly before we think about a return,” the physio explained.

The injury was the main topic of concern after Pakistan’s eight‑wicket defeat in the opening ODI. Despite bowlers restricting Bangladesh to 210, Pakistan’s batting lineup collapsed and fell short. Talat’s absence was immediately felt as he had contributed both with bat and ball in recent matches.

Fans and teammates took to social media to wish Talat a speedy recovery, with many praising his commitment and effort on the field.

Pakistan will now assess replacement options ahead of the second ODI as they look to regroup after the injury setback.
