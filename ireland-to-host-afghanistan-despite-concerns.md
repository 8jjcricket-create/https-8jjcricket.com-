# [Ireland to Host Afghanistan Despite Concerns Over Controversial Issues](https://8jjcricket.com/news/ireland-to-host-afghanistan-despite-concerns-tHskrr)

Cricket Ireland has confirmed that it will host Afghanistan for an upcoming One Day International (ODI) series, despite growing concerns surrounding political and ethical issues linked to the Afghanistan regime.

The series, scheduled for August, will feature multiple ODI matches hosted in Belfast and other venues, marking a significant bilateral commitment between the two sides.

The decision has sparked debate within the cricketing world, particularly due to concerns over the treatment of women in Afghanistan. Several major cricket boards, including those from Australia and England, have previously avoided bilateral series against Afghanistan on similar grounds.

Cricket Ireland chief executive Sarah Keane acknowledged the “moral discomfort” surrounding the decision but stated that the board believes continuing engagement is in the best interest of the sport. She also emphasised that Ireland intends to support Afghan women’s cricket by engaging with the exiled women’s team.

Officials stressed that the decision was not driven by financial or legal factors, but rather by a broader view of maintaining cricketing ties while also keeping important issues in focus.

The move highlights the complex balance between sporting relations and global political concerns, as cricket continues to navigate sensitive issues beyond the field.
