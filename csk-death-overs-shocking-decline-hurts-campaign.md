# CSK Death Overs: Shocking Decline Hurts IPL 2026 Campaign

The **Chennai Super Kings (CSK)** have encountered a troubling trend in **IPL 2026**, with their **death‑overs bowling performance deteriorating drastically**, significantly hurting their overall campaign.

One of the most striking issues has been CSK’s inability to contain runs in the final phases of matches. In matches against teams like **Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB)**, CSK has conceded huge totals late in the innings — including **78 runs in the last four overs** — putting enormous pressure on both bowlers and batters. This has made defending high scores extremely difficult for the five‑time champions and contributed to multiple losses. 

Analysts point out that CSK’s **death‑overs economy rate is among the worst in the league so far**, with bowlers struggling to execute yorkers consistently and often offering full or loose deliveries that get punished by power‑hitters. Their lack of variation and precision in these crucial overs has allowed opposition teams to accelerate freely, even after steady bowling early in the match. 
In their recent defeat to RCB, CSK’s bowlers started well by restricting the opposition in the early phases, but ultimately **lost control in the late overs**, leading to a massive total of **250/3** — a target CSK couldn’t chase down despite spirited efforts with the bat. 

CSK’s declining death‑over execution is compounded by injuries and personnel changes that have disrupted the balance of their bowling unit. With key death‑overs specialists unavailable or out of form, the team has struggled to find the right combinations to stop opposition accelerations at the end of games.

Unless CSK can find solutions to this specific weakness — whether through tactical changes, player form improvements, or personnel adjustments — their campaign in IPL 2026 could continue to be defined by late‑innings collapses and costly conceded runs.
