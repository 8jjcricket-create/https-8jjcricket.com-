# [Sarfaraz Ahmed Retires From International Cricket After Illustrious Pakistan Career](https://8jjcricket.com/news/sarfaraz-ahmed-retires-from-international-cricket-after-illustrious-pakistan-career-RRPc3L)

Pakistan’s former captain and wicket‑keeper batter **Sarfaraz Ahmed** has announced his retirement from international cricket, ending a career that spanned more than a decade at the highest level.

Sarfaraz made his international debut in 2007 and went on to become one of Pakistan’s most respected leaders, captaining the national side across formats. He was a central figure in Pakistan’s **2017 ICC Champions Trophy victory**, playing key innings and inspiring his teammates throughout the tournament.

Known for his combative batting style and sharp wicket‑keeping skills, Sarfaraz scored crucial runs in pressure situations and was often praised for his leadership during challenging tours. His captaincy also saw Pakistan record notable Test and ODI wins on the world stage.

In addition to his Champions Trophy success, Sarfaraz contributed to Pakistan’s competitive performances in Asia Cups, ODI series victories and T20 campaigns. His tactical acumen and fearless approach earned him admiration from teammates, opponents and fans alike.

After retiring from international duty, Sarfaraz expressed his appreciation for the support he received throughout his career and said he looks forward to exploring coaching and mentoring roles in cricket.

Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB) officials paid tribute to Sarfaraz’s contributions, acknowledging his role in nurturing young talent and his influence on the next generation of Pakistani cricketers.

The wicket‑keeper batter thanked fans for their unwavering support and hinted at continuing involvement in the sport at the domestic and franchise levels.
