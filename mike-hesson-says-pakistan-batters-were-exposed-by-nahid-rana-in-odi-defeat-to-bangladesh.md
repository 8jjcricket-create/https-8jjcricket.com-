# [Mike Hesson Says Pakistan Batters Were Exposed by Nahid Rana in ODI Defeat to Bangladesh](https://8jjcricket.com/news/mike-hesson-says-pakistan-batters-were-exposed-by-nahid-rana-in-odi-defeat-to-bangladesh-2L9ZtX)

Pakistan head coach Mike Hesson admitted that his side’s batting lineup was exposed after a crushing defeat to Bangladesh in the opening ODI of the series in Dhaka.

Bangladesh secured a commanding **eight-wicket victory**, with young fast bowler Nahid Rana producing a devastating spell that dismantled Pakistan’s top order. Rana claimed **five wickets for 24 runs**, triggering a collapse that saw Pakistan bowled out for just 114 in 30.4 overs.

Speaking after the match, Hesson acknowledged that the batting performance fell well below expectations.

“It’s a pretty disappointing batting performance. It wasn’t just the youngsters — we were exposed against some very good bowling,” Hesson said.

Rana’s fiery pace and aggressive line caused major problems for Pakistan’s batters. The Bangladeshi pacer struck repeatedly during the early stages of the innings, removing several key players and putting Pakistan under pressure almost immediately.

Hesson praised Rana’s performance, describing it as exceptional and crediting the bowler for maintaining discipline with his pace and accuracy.

“The wicket played pretty well. Nahid Rana was exceptional,” Hesson added.

Pakistan fielded **four ODI debutants** in the match as part of a broader rebuilding effort ahead of the **2027 ICC Cricket World Cup**. However, the experiment struggled to deliver immediate results, with the debutants contributing only a small portion of the team’s total.

Despite the heavy defeat, Hesson defended the young players and stressed the importance of giving emerging cricketers opportunities at the international level.

He emphasized that the team must learn quickly from the loss and improve in the remaining matches of the series.

Bangladesh chased the modest target comfortably, reaching **115 for 2 in just over 15 overs**, taking an early lead in the three-match ODI series.
